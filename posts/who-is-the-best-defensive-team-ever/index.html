Thunder Make Their Case as the Greatest Defensive Team of All Time

NBA
Basketball
Professional
Author

Trey Stevens

Published

November 16, 2025

The 2024-2025 Oklahoma City Thunder weren’t only elite on defense. They played defense like they were pissed just seeing the other side team try to score.

This wasn’t no small improvement. This wasn’t a “young team taking a step.” This was a team that shook things up in NBA history. And by the end of the season, one question kept resurfacing:

Did we just watch the most dominant defense the NBA has ever seen?

Spoiler alert: You could make that case, loudly.

And then the postseason arrived. Turns out the playoffs made their case even stronger.

But before the playoffs arrived, games showed exactly just how overwhelming OKC was, the regular season set the groundwork for what came next.

The Thunder didn’t just force mistakes, they forced them more than anyone. Their pressure turned simple actions into broken possessions. Their anticipation turned passes into turnovers. And their quickness in securing loose balls altered the game’s tempo.

Even when teams avoided turnovers, scoring against the Thunder remained a struggle. Oklahoma City’s disciplined rotations and relentless shot-contesting dropped opponents’ field goal percentage to the lowest in the league. Shot selection dried up, spacing was crowded and both midrange attempts and layups were heavily contested. Every possession felt uncomfortable, as the Thunder dictated the pace and forced opponents into low-percentage shots at every possession at every turn.
